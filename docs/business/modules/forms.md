# Forms and Operational Checklists

**Audience:** Operations, Warehouse, Compliance  
**Plain-language guide to:** Using Zap's digital checklists and structured data-collection forms

---

## What are Forms?

**Forms** in Zap are digital checklists or structured input sheets that teams fill in as part of regular operational tasks. They replace paper-based or ad hoc spreadsheet checklists with tracked, searchable digital records.

Examples of where Forms are used:
- Daily warehouse opening checklist
- Pre-dispatch quality check
- Incoming goods inspection report
- Cold-chain compliance sign-off

---

## Real-world example: Pre-dispatch quality check

> **Scenario:** Before a consignment of 60 boxes leaves the warehouse, a supervisor must run a quality check on a sample.

1. The supervisor opens Zap and selects the "Pre-Dispatch QC" form.
2. The form lists all checkpoints: box sealing, label presence, temperature (if cold chain), etc.
3. Each checkpoint is ticked — or flagged with a note if there is an issue.
4. On submission, the form is saved against the consignment record with the supervisor's name, timestamp, and result.
5. If an issue was flagged, the relevant manager receives a notification and the dispatch is held pending resolution.

---

## What you can do with Forms

- **Create a form template** — define the checkpoints and questions once
- **Fill in a form** — step through the checklist and submit
- **View form history** — see all submitted forms, who submitted them, and when
- **Search and filter** — find forms by date, type, or linked PO/consignment
- **Export form data** — download responses to Excel for reporting
