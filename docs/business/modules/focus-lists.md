# Focus Lists

**Audience:** Merchandising, Category Managers, Sales  
**Plain-language guide to:** Curating personal or team shortlists of SKUs to track and prioritise

---

## What is a Focus List?

A **Focus List** is a personally curated shortlist of products that a team member wants to track closely. It is similar to a "watchlist" or "favourites" — you add the SKUs you care about most, and they are always just one click away.

Focus lists are useful for:
- Category managers tracking the performance of a specific range
- Sales reps managing a particular buyer's assortment
- Operations tracking SKUs with recurring stock issues

---

## Real-world example: A buyer's product shortlist

> **Scenario:** An account manager is responsible for the Zepto account. Zepto stocks 120 of your SKUs, but 15 are high-velocity and need close attention.

1. The account manager creates a Focus List called "Zepto — Core 15."
2. They add the 15 SKUs to the list.
3. Each morning, they open the Focus List instead of searching the entire catalogue — and can instantly see stock levels, pending inbound, and any flags for those 15 products.

---

## What you can do with Focus Lists

- **Create a focus list** — name it and add any SKUs from the listings
- **Edit the list** — add or remove SKUs as priorities change
- **View stock and status** — see key metrics for all SKUs in the list at a glance
- **Share a list** — share a focus list with a colleague
- **Delete a list** — when it is no longer needed
